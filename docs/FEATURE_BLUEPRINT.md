# KIDORA Quest Feature Blueprint

## Pedagogical Model

KIDORA Quest uses a six-stage integrated English learning cycle:

1. Hear: listening to sounds, words, instructions, and story clues.
2. Move: tapping, sorting, matching, and building meaning through action.
3. Say: recording words and sentences as voice evidence.
4. Read: matching letters, words, pictures, phrases, and short sentences.
5. Create: drawing, tracing, labelling, and sentence building.
6. Share: saving multimodal evidence in a Living Storybook portfolio.

## AI Integration Model

The app includes local AI-simulated logic for safe prototype use:

- QuestWeaver AI generates integrated quests from teacher-selected theme, level, vocabulary, and sentence frame.
- StorySeed logic creates short storyworld missions.
- EchoMirror AI supports voice evidence and child-safe feedback.
- GrowthPath AI analyzes skill scores and provides adaptive recommendations.
- Home Practice AI creates five-minute parent practice cards.
- Report AI generates professional teacher and parent progress summaries.

## Safety Logic

- No open child chatbot.
- AI-generated quests enter a safety queue before learner access.
- Teacher/admin approval is required before publication.
- Parent-facing reports are positive and developmentally appropriate.
- Data is stored locally in the browser in this prototype.

## Research Evidence

The app logs:

- completed quest steps
- skill progress
- voice evidence events
- drawing evidence events
- portfolio saved events
- teacher observation notes
- AI-generated reports
- completion timestamps

CSV export is available in the Teacher Research Cockpit and Admin Analytics Export.
